I am an Assistant Professor of English Language Teaching and Learning at Brigham Young University–Hawaii. My work sits at the intersection of language education, motivation research, and the responsible integration of artificial intelligence in learning environments.

My current work centers on the application of generative AI in language education — from automated feedback systems and differentiated materials development to learner-facing tools built in collaboration with preservice teachers.

Active Research

01
Lang Lang

A desktop language learning app built with Electron and powered by Claude AI. Combines spaced repetition vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation, reading passages, and writing practice across multiple languages — adapting to each learner's progress through intelligent review scheduling, streak tracking, and proficiency-matched content.

02
Dynamic Written Corrective Feedback

A web-based classroom tool that automates Written Corrective Feedback for ESL writing instruction. Using Claude AI, DWCF annotates student drafts with standardized grammar error codes — encouraging active self-correction. Personalized grammar worksheets are generated from each student's individual error patterns.

03
LIFT Lab

Language Innovation and Futures in TESOL. A mobile innovation hub within the BYU–Hawaii TESOL program where faculty and preservice teachers build, experiment, and imagine the future of English language education — preparing educators not only to use AI responsibly, but to actively shape it.

04
AI Agents for Differentiated Materials

Exploring agentic AI as a collaborative partner in language materials development. Traditional publishing economics constrain differentiation at scale. By working alongside educators, agentic AI systems capable of planning, iterating, and executing complex authoring tasks may finally make genuinely differentiated materials widely achievable.
