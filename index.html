01 — About

I am an Assistant Professor of English Language Teaching and Learning at Brigham Young University–Hawaii. My work sits at the intersection of language education, motivation research, and the responsible integration of artificial intelligence in learning environments.

My current work centers on the application of generative AI in language education — from automated feedback systems and differentiated materials development to learner-facing tools built in collaboration with preservice teachers. I am particularly interested in how AI can be integrated responsibly, in ways that deepen rather than shortcut the learning process.

Current Research Projects

Lang Lang

A desktop language learning app built with Electron and powered by Claude AI. Lang Lang combines spaced repetition vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation, reading passages, and writing practice across multiple languages — adapting to each learner's progress through intelligent review scheduling, streak tracking, and proficiency-matched content.

Dynamic Written Corrective Feedback (DWCF)

A web-based classroom tool that automates Written Corrective Feedback for ESL writing instruction. Using Claude AI, DWCF annotates student drafts with standardized grammar error codes — encouraging active self-correction rather than passive editing. A structured weekly cycle of drafting, feedback, revision, and reflection is paired with personalized grammar worksheets generated from each student's individual error patterns.

LIFT Lab — Language Innovation and Futures in TESOL

A mobile innovation hub within the BYU–Hawaii TESOL program where faculty and preservice teachers build, experiment, and imagine the future of English language education. The LIFT Lab harnesses AI agents to design educational technology, interactive platforms, and pedagogical materials — preparing future educators not only to use AI responsibly, but to actively shape it within the field.

AI Agents for Differentiated Language Learning Materials

This project explores agentic AI as a collaborative partner in language materials development. Traditional publishing economics constrain differentiation — producing tiered texts, scaffolded tasks, and culturally adapted content at scale is logistically and financially prohibitive. By working alongside educators, agentic AI systems capable of planning, iterating, and executing complex authoring tasks may finally make genuinely differentiated, contextually responsive materials widely achievable.
